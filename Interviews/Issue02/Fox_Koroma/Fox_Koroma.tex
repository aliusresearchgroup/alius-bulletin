% \section*{Abstract}
% This interview with Kieran Fox examines spontaneous thinking as an umbrella covering mind-wandering, dreaming, and meditation, and its connections to creativity and well-being. Fox recounts how his work with Kalina Christoff led him to map the parallels between mind-wandering and dreaming. The conversation lays out the spontaneous thought framework, in which cognition sits on a spectrum between totally constrained thought and disorganized thinking approaching acute psychosis, with mind-wandering, dreaming, and creative thinking falling between these extremes. Fox argues that most mind-wandering proceeds below meta-awareness and compares the stream of inner thought to other perceptual channels filtered by attention. He treats mind-wandering and dreaming as matters of degree, with sensory decoupling and increased activity in default, memory, and visual networks accounting for the more immersive character of dream experience. Further sections examine the determinants of thought content, including dominant visual, auditory, and somatosensory modalities, mildly positive average emotional valence, and Eric Klinger's notion of current concerns. The conversation turns to mindfulness meditation as a way to investigate spontaneous thought without disturbing it, citing work on long-term practitioners that locates the medial temporal lobe and default network among the antecedent activations of arising thoughts. Fox then considers the adaptive value of mind-wandering, flow states, and creativity as a special case of focus on current concerns, before treating post-traumatic stress disorder and depressive rumination as distortions of spontaneous thinking. The interview closes on neuroimaging evidence linking mind-wandering to the default network and on future work into the stability and malleability of thought patterns.\par
